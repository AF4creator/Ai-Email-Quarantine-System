Frequently Asked Questions

A Gmail App Password is a unique 16-digit code that allows legacy or third-party applications (which do not support "Sign in with Google") to securely access your Gmail account without using your main password.
First, enable 2-Step Verification for your Google account. Then visit the Google App Passwords page, enter a name for the app (for example, "Email Scanner" or "Python Script"), and click Create. Copy the generated 16-digit password immediately and use it in place of your regular password inside the application.
The App Passwords option may not appear if 2-Step Verification is not enabled, if Advanced Protection is turned on, or if you are using a work or school account where the administrator has disabled this feature.
IMAP (Internet Message Access Protocol) allows Gmail to synchronize emails across multiple devices in real time. It is usually enabled by default, but some legacy or third-party integrations may require manual verification.
Open Gmail in a web browser, click the Settings gear icon, choose "See all settings," then open the "Forwarding and POP/IMAP" tab. Under IMAP Access, select Enable IMAP and click Save Changes.
The application uses IMAP over SSL/TLS on port 993 to encrypt all email data during transmission. To meet 2026 security standards, OAuth 2.0 authentication is recommended, which uses short-lived access tokens instead of storing your main email password.
